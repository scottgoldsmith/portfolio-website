An attorney turned programmer

I became a software developer after practicing law for three years. Most thought I was crazy, but I was happy to be changing careers and getting back into the habit of solving problems.

I became an attorney to solve problems for people, and once my job no longer entailed that, I knew something had to change. Programming is all about solving problems, each and every day and I love that. I love what I do now and I love creating things and helping people solve their problems.
